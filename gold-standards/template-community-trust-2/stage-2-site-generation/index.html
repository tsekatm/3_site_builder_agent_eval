Who We Are

Community-Owned, Community-Led

Khula Community Trust is a development-focused NPO working in Limpopo, Mpumalanga, and the Eastern Cape. Our programmes address food security, early childhood development, and adult literacy. We work hand-in-hand with traditional leaders and local government to ensure our interventions are community-owned and sustainable.

Partner With Us

Our Focus Areas

Food Gardens

Establishing community and household food gardens in 45 villages, providing nutrition training and sustainable farming techniques to over 3,000 families.

Early Childhood Development

Building and equipping ECD centres in underserved areas. We have established 18 centres serving 1,400 children with trained practitioners and nutritious meals.

Skills Training & Literacy

Adult literacy and vocational skills programmes have reached 2,200 community members, with accredited training in sewing, construction, and digital skills.

Our Impact

Rooted in Community, Growing Together

Since 2010, Khula Community Trust has impacted over 25,000 people across three provinces. Our food gardens produce an average of 2 tonnes of vegetables per village per year, reducing household food insecurity by 40%.
